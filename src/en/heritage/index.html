03 — Heritage

Geology produces the green. Culture gives it a name.

Colombia supplies 90% of the world's emerald market. Nineteen of the twenty highest-priced emeralds ever sold at auction come from the same geological triangle — Muzo, Chivor, Coscuez — a geological-cultural coincidence no other source reproduces. Here is why.

01

Geology

Sedimentary, not igneous. That's the whole difference.

Everywhere else in the world, emeralds form in igneous or metamorphic rock — in Zambia, Brazil, Afghanistan, they are born of volcanic heat and pressure. In Colombia, the process is unique: hydrothermal fluids carrying chromium and vanadium passed through the sedimentary rock of the Eastern Andes some 40 million years ago, cooling slowly in limestone fissures.

That slowness produces what Christie's and Sotheby's call "the classic green" — a deep, slightly bluish saturation impossible to replicate in an igneous deposit. It is the geological signature the global market has recognized since the sixteenth century.

The Colombian green is not a colour. It's a geological DNA.

02

The three mines

Three mines, three geologies, three centuries of distinct history.

1539 → today
Muzo

Five centuries uninterrupted.

First mentioned in the Spanish archives in 1539, the Muzo mine has never stopped producing. The conquistadors reported early on that the Muiscas were already extracting stones there well before their arrival. Muzo's emeralds are the world's most saturated — the deep, warm, slightly bluish green that serves as the auction-house reference.

1675 → 1896
Chivor

Abandoned, lost, then found again.

The Chivor mine, known to the Muiscas as Somondoco — "the god of the green stones" — was abandoned in 1675 under colonial pressure. It remained forgotten for more than two centuries. In 1896, Colombian engineer Don Francisco Restrepo rediscovered it by following the trace of an ancient document. Chivor's emeralds stand out for their crystalline bluish-green and brilliance — a signature that early-twentieth-century European collectors fought over.

20th century
Coscuez

The character of the yellow-green stone.

Rediscovered in the modern era, the Coscuez mine produces an intense green with yellow undertones — a signature less sought after than Muzo, but unique, and today prized precisely for its character. Coscuez stones are often smaller but more vibrant, ideal for pavé work and multi-stone pieces.

03

Muisca mythology

Before Spain, there was Fura.

The Muisca people occupied the Colombian Andean plateau long before the Spanish arrived in 1537. Their cosmology granted green stones a sacred status: emeralds were offerings to sacred lakes, mountains, and ancestors. The legend of Fura and Tena — the mortal and her lover, turned into twin peaks by the god Áre's wrath — makes emeralds the tears Fura shed, petrified into stone.

The Fura mountain rises today 840 metres above the Minero valley, where Muzo's emeralds are mined. Science confirms the geology; the legend precedes and outlives everything.

04

Modern era

19 of the 20 highest-priced emeralds ever sold at auction.

Of the twenty emeralds that have crossed US $100,000 per carat in Christie's and Sotheby's catalogues over the past forty years, nineteen are Colombian. The most remarkable: the Rockefeller Emerald, 18.04 carats, sold in 2017 for US $5.5 million — $305,000 per carat. Origin: Muzo.

Auctions don't lie. The market has known for 500 years. — Auction-house consensus

05

Our standard

Every stone has a documented origin. No exception.

01

Traceable provenance. Every stone comes with documentation of its origin mine. We don't sell anything for which we can't name the mine — Muzo, Chivor, Coscuez.

02

Recognized certifications. Our certifying partners include the GIA (Gemological Institute of America) and IGAC Colombia. Exceptional stones are accompanied by their report.

03

Visible inclusions. Colombian emeralds naturally have inclusions — what the trade calls the jardin. This is not a defect: it is proof of sedimentary and natural origin. We don't retouch our photos to hide them.

04

Oiling disclosed. Light oiling (natural-oil impregnation to fill surface inclusions) is an ancient, standard industry practice. Every piece whose stone has been oiled states so explicitly.

05

Direct relationship with Colombia. We don't route through Tel Aviv, Bangkok, or Geneva. Our network is Colombian, from the mine to Quebec.
