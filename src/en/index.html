03 — Mythological origin

Fura and Tena, or why the stone weeps.

Fifteen centuries ago, the Muisca people of the Andean plateau told of Fura, the mortal, and Tena, her lover. When Fura betrayed him, the god Áre's wrath turned the pair into two mountains. Fura rises 840 metres above the Minero valley in Colombia. Tena stands beside her. The tears Fura shed — legend says — became the emeralds scattered across the Muzo mineland. Fifteen hundred years later, science has confirmed the geology. The legend survives in every stone pulled from Colombian earth.
